SCOPE:
Design and prototype a multi-building autonomous emergency response system (A-RES) utilizing integrated sensor hubs (combining structural damage detection, occupancy detection, and fire/gas risk assessment) located at the nodes of the building infrastructure by employing a risk-based sampling technique. This design includes an IoT network framework enabling instant inter-building data transfer, an active damper system to safeguard building structures, a modular interior space design optimized to ensure adequate sensor coverage, and physical casing of the sensor hubs, and a priority-weighted optimization system to autonomously assess which buildings are in most need of emergency response resources-this has been verified via a seismic event simulation.

Business Proposal for Integrated Sensor Hub Supply and Development
Client: Istanbul Beykent University – Faculty of Engineering and Architecture
Project: A-RES: Autonomous Earthquake Response System (Self-Adaptive Smart Building Network for Earthquake Resilience)
Date: 30.04.2026

Scope of Work
We propose to design, develop, and deliver the Integrated Sensor Hub system for the A-RES project; a multi-building autonomous earthquake response network. Each Integrated Sensor Hub is a self-contained unit combining three core detection modules:

Structural Damage Detection: Accelerometers measuring building vibration and displacement in real time
Occupancy Tracking: Thermal and motion sensors detecting the number of occupants per zone
Fire/Gas Risk Monitoring: Smoke and heat sensors identifying secondary disaster risks immediately after seismic events

Sensor Hubs will be placed exclusively at critical structural nodes, determined by the Civil Engineering team using risk-based sampling methodology, eliminating the need for full-room coverage and reducing overall hardware costs.


Details of Supply and Services
Full design and fabrication of Integrated Sensor Hub units
IoT network integration for real-time data transmission between buildings
Development of the priority-weighted optimization algorithm for autonomous emergency resource allocation
Physical casing design (impact-resistant, aesthetically considered) by the Industrial Design team
Adaptive damping system design and integration by the Mechanical Engineering team
Interior layout planning to eliminate sensor blind spots by the Interior Architecture team
System commissioning and simulation testing against seismic event scenarios
Full technical documentation and project report

3. ECONOMIC ANALYSIS
Turkey's 2023 earthquakes caused $34.2 billion in direct physical damage according to the World Bank, with total costs reaching $150 billion when indirect losses are included. Residential buildings alone accounted for 53% of all damage, over $18 billion. Istanbul, sitting on the North Anatolian Fault, faces a similar or greater risk given its density and population.
Research shows that every $1 invested in disaster resilience systems generates up to $35 in public value when disasters occur. IoT-based earthquake early warning systems have been proven to significantly reduce response time, minimize casualties, and lower post-disaster recovery costs. Japan's national earthquake warning system, the world's most advanced, cost $1 billion, while IoT-based alternatives like A-RES demonstrate that the same life-saving capability can be achieved at a fraction of the cost through smart sensor networks.
The A-RES system directly addresses the 3 largest cost categories of earthquake damage:

For Istanbul specifically, a scalable system like A-RES deployed across connected buildings represents a high-value, low-cost public safety investment relative to the billions in potential damage it aims to prevent.

5. FEASIBILITY ANALYSIS
5.1 Technical Feasibility
All components selected for the A-RES Integrated Sensor Hub are commercially available, widely used in IoT projects, and compatible with the ESP32 microcontroller platform. The IoT-based approach has been validated in academic literature as technically feasible for earthquake early warning applications. The 6-department team covers all required engineering disciplines, ensuring full technical coverage across structural, software, mechanical, interior, and design domains.
5.2 Financial Feasibility
The total project budget of ₺10,500 is sufficient to build and test 1 fully functional Integrated Sensor Hub prototype. The component cost of approximately ₺3,600 per hub leaves adequate remaining budget for software development (₺2,000), additional prototyping materials (₺3,000), and documentation (₺500). No external funding is required beyond university resource provision.
5.3 Time Feasibility
The project timeline runs from 22 April to 15 June 2026, a total of 8 weeks. Based on the Gantt Chart, all 6 design tasks run in parallel during weeks 3–4, followed by 2 weeks of prototyping, 1 week of testing, and 1 week of final reporting. This schedule is achievable given the team size of 17 members across 6 departments.
